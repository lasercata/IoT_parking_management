    \begin{indt}{\section{Chosen Scenario}}% {{{1
        This project focuses on the design of an IoT-based smart parking system dedicated to people with disabilities. The objective is to ensure fair usage of reserved parking spaces, prevent fraud related to authorization cloning, and improve accessibility by providing real-time availability and booking capabilities.

        Each parking space is equipped with an IoT node capable of detecting vehicle presence and verifying the validity of a disabled parking authorization. The system continuously monitors parking activity and communicates with a centralized backend that manages authorizations, detects anomalies, and informs law enforcement when violations are detected.

        In addition, an interface allows authorized users to visualize nearby available parking spaces, reserve a spot up to one hour in advance, and receive feedback on their parking session. 
    \end{indt}% }}}1

    \begin{indt}{\section{Sensing and Identification Technologies}} %{{{1
        \begin{indt}{\subsection{Vehicle Presence Detection}} %{{{2
            \begin{table}[H]% {{{3
                \centering

                \begin{tabular}{p{3.5cm} p{5cm} p{6cm}}
                    \hline
                    \textbf{Technology}
                    & \textbf{Principle}
                    & \textbf{Remarks}
                    \\

                    \hline
                    Ultrasonic sensor
                    & Measures the distance between the sensor and the underside of a vehicle
                    & Low cost and simple to integrate; performance may be affected by rain, snow, or dirt accumulation
                    \\

                    Magnetic sensor
                    & Detects disturbances in the Earthâ€™s magnetic field caused by a vehicle
                    & Very low power consumption, highly robust, widely adopted in commercial smart parking systems
                    \\

                    Infrared (IR) sensor
                    & Detects reflected infrared radiation from nearby objects
                    & Low cost; susceptible to false detections due to sunlight, shadows, or surface contamination
                    \\

                    Camera-based detection
                    & Uses image processing algorithms to detect vehicle presence
                    & High detection accuracy; high cost, high power consumption, and significant privacy and legal constraints
                    \\

                    \hline
                \end{tabular}

                \caption{Vehicle Presence Detection Technologies}
                \label{tab:presence}
            \end{table}% }}}3

            For this project, a \textbf{Magnetic sensor} is selected as the primary solution, with Infrared (IR) sensor and Ultrasonic sensor considered as a viable alternative, due to their low power consumption and robustness.
        \end{indt} %}}}2

        \begin{indt}{\subsection{Authorization Identification}} %{{{2
            \begin{table}[H]% {{{3
                \centering

                \begin{tabular}{p{3.5cm} p{9cm}}
                    \hline
                    \textbf{Technology}
                    & \textbf{Justification}
                    \\

                    \hline
                    RFID / NFC
                    & Short-range communication prevents remote fraud, low power consumption, mature and widely deployed technology, no dependency on smartphones, and easy integration into low-power IoT nodes.
                    \\

                    Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE)
                    & Authorization stored in a mobile application; no physical card required; however, depends on smartphone availability, battery level, and user interaction.
                    \\

                    License Plate Recognition (LPR)
                    & Automatic vehicle identification without user action; high computational cost, higher power consumption, privacy concerns, and legal restrictions limit its applicability.
                    \\

                    \hline
                \end{tabular}

                \caption{Authorization Identification Technologies}
                \label{tab:auth}
            \end{table}% }}}3

            Based on this comparison, the \textbf{RFID/NFC-based solution} is selected for this project due to its simplicity, robustness, low energy consumption, and minimal privacy impact.
        \end{indt} %}}}2
